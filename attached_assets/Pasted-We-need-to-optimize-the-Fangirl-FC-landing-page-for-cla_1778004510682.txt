We need to optimize the Fangirl FC landing page for clarity, conversion, and TikTok traffic.

Context:
The current landing page has a strong visual style: dark background, pink/purple gradients, bold typography, glassmorphism cards, and playful World Cup fangirl energy.
Do NOT redesign the whole visual identity.
Keep the existing color palette, typography direction, spacing style, rounded cards, gradients, and overall aesthetic.

Current problem:
The landing page looks good, but the user flow is not clear enough.
A TikTok user should understand within 3 seconds:
1. what this is
2. what they get
3. what button to click

Main goal:
Make the landing page immediately clear and action-driven:
“Take a quick quiz → get your Fangirl Card → send it to your bestie.”

Important:
Do not modify the quiz logic.
Do not modify the card generator.
Do not modify FanCard.
Do not modify authentication.
Do not modify download/export.
Do not change routes unless absolutely necessary.
This is a landing page UX/content/layout optimization only.

---

## Required landing page structure

### 1. Hero section — make it clearer

Current headline is:
“Find your World Cup fan personality”

Replace with a clearer, more TikTok-friendly headline:

“Find your Fangirl Type in 60 seconds 💖”

Subtitle:
“Take the quiz. Get your card. Send it to your bestie.”

Add a small trust/friction line under the CTA:
“No signup • 5 questions • Just for fun”

The hero must make the flow obvious immediately.

---

### 2. CTA hierarchy — one primary action

Current issue:
There are two competing CTAs:
- “Find my fan type”
- “Make my card”

This creates confusion.

Required fix:
Keep ONE dominant primary CTA above the fold:

Primary button:
“Find my fan type →”

Secondary action:
If “Make my card” is still needed, make it visually secondary and smaller, below the main CTA:
“Already have a result? Make my card”

Do not make both buttons look equally important.

---

### 3. Above-the-fold layout

On first screen, user should see:
- Logo / brand
- Clear headline
- Short subtitle
- One main CTA
- Small “No signup • 5 questions • 60 sec” reassurance
- A preview of the cards

Add a small label above the card mockup:
“Your result will look like this 👇”

The card preview should support the CTA, not just be decorative.

---

### 4. Move identity examples higher

The “Which one are you?” identity section is useful.
Move it higher, directly after the hero/card preview.

Purpose:
Users should instantly understand that results are personality types.

Improve the section title:
“Which fangirl type are you?”

If the app currently has 10 personality presets, do not hardcode “6 fan identities”.
Use the correct dynamic count from the existing data source if possible.
If there are still only 6 quiz identities, keep it consistent everywhere.
Avoid mismatches like “6 identities” in one place and 10 presets elsewhere.

---

### 5. Simplify “How it works”

Keep the section, but make it shorter and more direct.

Use exactly this flow:

1. Answer 5 questions  
   “Tell us how you really watch football.”

2. Get your Fangirl Card  
   “Your type, vibe, score, and team mood.”

3. Send it to your bestie  
   “Compare results and start the group chat drama.”

Keep the design consistent with existing cards.

---

### 6. Reduce confusing locked section prominence

The “Unlocked Identities” section currently appears too early and may confuse new users.

Required:
- Move it lower on the page OR visually reduce its importance.
- It should not distract from the main CTA.
- If shown before the quiz, explain it better:
  “Unlock more types after your first result.”

Do not let “0/6 unlocked” be a confusing first impression.

---

### 7. Testimonials section

Keep testimonials, but make them supporting proof, not the main flow.

Improve heading:
“What girls are saying 💬”

Keep cards horizontally scrollable if already implemented.
Make sure no testimonial card is cut off awkwardly on desktop or mobile.

---

### 8. Navigation

The current top nav has many pills.
For mobile and small screens:
- Keep it horizontally scrollable if needed.
- But do not let it dominate the hero.
- The primary CTA “Take Quiz” must remain clear.

Do not add more nav items.

---

### 9. Copy style rules

Use short, TikTok-native copy.
Avoid long explanations.

Tone:
- fun
- direct
- girly
- shareable
- not corporate

Good examples:
- “Find your Fangirl Type”
- “Send it to your bestie”
- “Which one are you?”
- “No signup • 5 questions • 60 sec”

Avoid:
- long paragraphs
- overly generic marketing text
- too many competing CTAs

---

### 10. Legal/brand wording safety

Avoid adding new official FIFA branding.
If possible, prefer generic copy like:
- “World Cup 2026”
- “Fan World Cup 2026”
- “World Cup fangirl energy”

Do not add official logos, official marks, or language that implies affiliation.

---
